Search Engine Optimization (SEO) is crucial for getting your content discovered. This blog template comes with built-in SEO features, but understanding how they work will help you maximize your blog's visibility.

## Built-in SEO Features

### Meta Tags

Every page automatically generates appropriate meta tags:

- **Title**: Uses your post title with site name
- **Description**: Uses your post description
- **Canonical URL**: Prevents duplicate content issues
- **Robots**: Controls indexing behavior

### OpenGraph & Twitter Cards

Social sharing is optimized with:

- OpenGraph tags for Facebook and LinkedIn
- Twitter Card tags for rich previews
- Automatic image sizing (1200x630)

### Schema.org Markup

Structured data helps search engines understand your content:

- `WebSite` schema for your homepage
- `BlogPosting` schema for each post
- `BreadcrumbList` for navigation
- `Person` schema for authors

## Best Practices

### 1. Write Descriptive Titles

Your title should:
- Be under 60 characters
- Include your primary keyword
- Be compelling and clickable

### 2. Craft Good Descriptions

Descriptions appear in search results:
- Keep them under 160 characters
- Summarize the content accurately
- Include a call to action

### 3. Use Proper Heading Structure

Structure your content with headings:
- One H1 (your title)
- H2 for main sections
- H3 for subsections

### 4. Optimize Images

For each image:
- Use descriptive filenames
- Add alt text
- Compress for web

### 5. Internal Linking

Link to related posts to:
- Keep readers engaged
- Help search engines discover content
- Build topic authority

## Monitoring Your SEO

Use these free tools to track your progress:

1. **Google Search Console**: Monitor indexing and search performance
2. **Google Analytics**: Track traffic and user behavior
3. **PageSpeed Insights**: Check performance scores

## Conclusion

SEO is an ongoing process. Focus on creating quality content, and the technical SEO provided by this template will handle the rest!
